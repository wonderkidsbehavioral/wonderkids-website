ABA Services Designed for Real Life

We help children ages 2–18 build practical skills at home, at school and throughout their communities.

Service 01

ABA In-Home Therapy

One-on-one ABA therapy in the familiar environment of home. Sessions are individualized and may address communication, social skills, play, self-care, transitions, daily routines, learning readiness and challenging behavior.

Possible areas of focus

  • Functional communication and requesting
  • Social interaction and play
  • Daily living and self-care routines
  • Emotional regulation and coping
  • Safety, transitions and flexibility
  • School readiness and independence

Service 02

Parent & Teacher Education and Training

Children make stronger progress when the adults around them understand what works. We teach practical strategies that can be used naturally during routines, learning activities, transitions and community outings.

  • Understanding behavior and motivation
  • Using positive reinforcement effectively
  • Supporting communication and independence
  • Preventing and responding to challenging behavior
  • Creating consistent expectations across settings
  • Collaborating with schools and caregivers
Request Parent or Teacher Training
Service 03

Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA)

An FBA is a structured process used to understand the function, or purpose, of behavior. Information may be gathered through interviews, observation and data review. The findings help guide individualized recommendations and behavior support planning.

  • Identify patterns, triggers and maintaining consequences
  • Clarify what the behavior may communicate
  • Develop prevention and teaching strategies
  • Select replacement skills that meet the same need
  • Track outcomes and adjust recommendations
